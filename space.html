Overview


Objective

Create an online store for everyone to find products quickly and easily.

Hypothesis

An in-person shopping experience that people enjoy is one for home decor. The stores stage everything nicely and enable shoppers to envision their space with many products. However, we all are busy and do not always have time to have that experience. There is a lot lost between physically being in a home decor store and shopping online, so the Space brand has a strong presence to bridge that gap. By maintaining a dynamic and modern style, it can bring the aesthetic feel shoppers want to their mobile device or computer without having to worry about lines, parking, or Karens.

My Process

The Brand

Mission

Space aims to give you a peaceful, enjoyable, and aesthetic shopping experience. We know how busy life is. Space brings the goods to you, wherever you may be, striving for the same immersive experience without the hassle. We keep it simple so you can fill the space with what you love and make it yours.

Color

Our colors were chosen carefully to convey a sleek and modern yet comfortable and approachable feel.

Typography

Our typography is a mixture of two different fonts to combine the simple feel with a modern edge. Monsterrat adds a bit of style while Lato keeps it clean and accessible.

Imagery

Images are simple and uncluttered. They have a pop of color for contrast. They focus on the product while still creating a warm environment.

Voice

The Space voice is warm, approachable, and casual. We want users to feel at ease and joyful while using our product. We are mature and trustworthy with a modern charm.

Outcomes and Lessons

What went well?

Researching and reading a variety of brand guidelines helped hone into building a brand for Space and really make it align with the vision described in the project brief.

Prototyping and getting user feedback early on helped me to not only, fix some errors but also gave me insight into trying new skills with animation.

What didn't go well?

I struggled a bit with the user flow and screens regarding the filters. Luckily, input from the user testing helped my focus on the flow. I plan on continuing to use feedback throughout the design process.

What did I learn?

I learned about time management and listening to my instincts. I spent a lot of time trying to design a logo. Nothing was clicking or making any visual sense. I felt like I was forcing it. I learned something about myself as designer: that I need to give myself space every now and then and not try to make a design decision when I am frustrated and not listening to my instincts.

Future Steps

I will continue to educate myself through reading articles and researching design ideas. I will continue to get user feedback early on and throughout the design process. I will learn more about animation and continue to build on my knowledge and skills in that area.
